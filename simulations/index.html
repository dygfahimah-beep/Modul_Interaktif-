🎓 Law of Large Numbers (LLN) Simulator

Adjust the sample size (n) to observe how the shape of the sampling distribution of the sample mean changes. As n increases, the distribution becomes narrower (less variability), more symmetric (approaching normal), and is centered at μ = 50, demonstrating the Law of Large Numbers.


Explanation: For small sample sizes (n < 30), the sampling distribution may still be skewed and shows greater variability around μ = 50. This indicates that the sample mean is more influenced by population variability (variance) and is less stable.
